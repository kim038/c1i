The Yellow-Throated Marten

The Yellow-Throated Marten, (Martes flavigula), is known through its distinctively golden and black fur. The Yellow-Throated Marten can be found from Central to East, and Southeast Asia, though they're most well known in the Himalyan region.

The Golden Child

The Yellow-Throated Marten is the largest marten species, and is known for its powerful, physical build. It has a black head and a white chin, with a predominantly yellow body, accompanies by black legs and a tail. Yellow-Throated Martens have a distinctly wide head, and the white chin only makes the face look more dense. Fixed with rounded ears on the sides of its head, the Yellow-Throated Marten has a face that looks alike to its cousin, the badger.

Behavior

The Yellow-Throated Marten is known for being particularly fearless of people. A bold predator, the Yellow-Throated Marten can be commonly seen hunting larger prey, which is how it recieved its reputation as a ferocious species. Targeting small derr, pheasants, and even red pandas if given the chance, the Yellow-Throated Marten is no stranger to fighting its own prey.
